# Public Thinker Lenses

Use these as public analysis styles for critique diversity, not as requests to impersonate a person or expose hidden reasoning. Prefer prompts like "apply a Feynman-style first-principles lens and summarize the rationale" over "be Feynman and show your thoughts."

Run `thinktank thinkers list` or `python3 -m athenaeum thinkers list` to inspect the lenses supported by the current checkout.

Panel prompt shape:

```text
Apply these public analysis lenses: Einstein, Feynman, Kahneman, Popper, Ostrom, Taleb.
For each lens, give: conclusion, key assumption, strongest objection, falsifier or test, evidence needed, confidence.
Do not include hidden chain of thought; provide concise rationale only.
```

Available lenses:

- Einstein: invariance, limit cases, simplifying assumptions, thought experiments.
- Feynman: first principles, plain-language reconstruction, sanity checks.
- Kahneman: base rates, bias audit, calibration, overconfidence checks.
- Popper: falsifiability, risky predictions, disconfirming tests.
- Ostrom: institutional fit, incentives, commons governance, local rule design.
- Taleb: tail risk, fragility, optionality, exposure, ruin constraints.
- Darwin: selection pressures, adaptation, variation, path dependence.
- Lovelace: formal procedure, symbolic systems, imaginative use cases.
- Turing: precise interfaces, computable tests, observable behavior.
- Franklin: evidence quality, measurement limits, structural fit.

Fusion rule: strip persona identity where practical and rank findings by evidence, specificity, and testability rather than by the thinker's reputation.
